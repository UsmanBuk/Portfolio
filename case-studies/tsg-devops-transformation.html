Problem

  • TSG's monolithic application suffered frequent downtime incidents costing an estimated £450K/year in lost revenue and customer churn.
  • Deployments were manual, error-prone, and limited to weekly releases — slowing feature delivery and exposing the business to extended outage windows.
  • Infrastructure costs were rising without corresponding capacity gains; the CTO needed a clear path to 2x throughput without proportional cost increase.

Solution

I architected and executed a phased migration from a monolithic Node.js application to a Kubernetes-orchestrated microservices architecture. I designed the service boundaries, implemented Terraform-managed infrastructure as code, and built CI/CD pipelines that enabled the team to ship daily with automated rollbacks and canary deployments.

Key engineering highlights

  • Led the microservices decomposition of a monolithic Node.js application, identifying 8 bounded contexts and implementing service-to-service communication via event-driven messaging — reducing cascading failures by 75%.
  • Designed and deployed Kubernetes clusters with Terraform IaC, implementing auto-scaling policies that doubled system capacity while reducing infrastructure costs by 20% (£180K+ annual avoidance).
  • Built comprehensive CI/CD pipelines with automated testing, canary deployments, and instant rollback capability — increasing deployment frequency from weekly to daily and reducing deployment failures by 85%.
  • Developed a Node.js/Express integration connecting Sage Intacct with IMP budgeting software, automating financial data exchange that previously required 15 hours/week of manual reconciliation.
  • Implemented observability across all services (structured logging, distributed tracing, health checks) enabling mean-time-to-detection of 2 minutes vs previous 45-minute average.
