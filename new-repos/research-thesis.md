# Research Thesis: Top 2 Revenue-Scale MVPs

I ran parallel research across healthcare operations, RevOps/finance, and monetization strategy, then ranked opportunities by:

- Pain intensity and urgency
- Clear budget owner
- Measurable ROI in pilot window
- Ability to ship a one-day MVP
- ACV expansion potential

## Top idea #1: DenialGuard (Healthcare Claims Recovery)

### Why it wins

- Tied directly to reclaimed revenue, not vanity metrics.
- Strong pilot narrative: "recover denied dollars in 14-30 days."
- Multiple expansion lanes: denial prevention, payer-specific playbooks, work queues.

### Initial GTM

1. Target RCM directors at mid-size provider groups.
2. Pilot on one denial code family + one payer.
3. Price on recovered dollars plus platform fee.

## Top idea #2: ShadowSpend (SaaS Cost Recovery)

### Why it wins

- CFO-visible savings with fast data access via CSV/SSO exports.
- Immediate value: duplicate stack and idle-license cuts.
- Expansion lanes: renewal optimization, contract benchmarking, procurement workflow automation.

### Initial GTM

1. Target finance + procurement in 150-1000 employee companies.
2. Run a 2-week "waste audit sprint."
3. Price with subscription + success fee on verified savings.

## Combined portfolio strategy

- **DenialGuard** is the revenue-recovery wedge in healthcare.
- **ShadowSpend** is the cost-recovery wedge in cross-industry B2B.
- Both can be sold with value-based pricing and converted from pilot to annual contracts after quantified impact.
